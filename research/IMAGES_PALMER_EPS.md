### B.2 Eugenia Kalnay -- NEW DOWNLOAD
- Source page: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Eugenia_Kalnay_Maniac_Talk.jpg
- Direct: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/51/Eugenia_Kalnay_Maniac_Talk.jpg
- License: **Public domain (NASA work)**
- Source: NASA Goddard Space Flight Center, "Maniac Talk" lecture series, 31 March 2015
- Caption: *Eugenia Kalnay at NASA Goddard, 2015. Director of NCEP's Environmental Modeling Center 1987-1997, she would launch the NCEP ensemble on 7 December 1992 -- just two weeks after Palmer's EPS. Photo: NASA, public domain.*

### B.3 NCEP spaghetti plot (Nov 2001) -- NEW DOWNLOAD
- Source page: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Nov192001h5spaghetti5640m.png
- Direct: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/44/Nov192001h5spaghetti5640m.png
- License: **Public domain (NOAA / U.S. government work)**
- Author: Environmental Modeling Center, NOAA
- Date: 19 November 2001
- Description: 5640 m isohypse at 500 hPa, 10 NCEP global ensemble members, 3.5-day forecast; areas of greatest uncertainty circled in red.
- Caption: *A 2001 NCEP global-ensemble spaghetti plot of the 500 hPa 5640 m contour from ten members, 3.5 days out. The "spaghetti" idiom -- closely-bunched lines mean confidence, spread means uncertainty -- became the operational language Palmer's EPS made routine across the North Atlantic. Image: NOAA / EMC, public domain.*

### B.4 NOAA contour-boxplots ensemble (2014) -- NEW DOWNLOAD
- Source page: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Contour_Boxplots_Weather_ensemble.jpg
- Direct: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/72/Contour_Boxplots_Weather_ensemble.jpg
- License: **Public domain (NOAA / U.S. government work)**
- Author: NOAA, Storm Prediction Center experimental ensemble visualization tool, 13 February 2014
- Caption: *An ensemble of isocontours rendered as a "spaghetti plot" from NOAA's experimental ensemble visualization tool. By the 2010s the visual language Palmer had championed in 1992 was standard issue at every operational center. Image: NOAA, public domain.*

### B.5 WRF Hurricane Rita ensemble track plot -- NEW DOWNLOAD (optional)
- Source page: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:WRF_rita_spread2.jpg
- Direct: https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d5/WRF_rita_spread2.jpg
- License: **Public domain (NASA)**
- Author: Prof. Robert Fovell (UCLA) and Dr. Hui Su (JPL); uploaded 5 March 2010 from a 22 Sept 2005 simulation
- Caption: *WRF ensemble simulations of Hurricane Rita's track (2005), with NHC cone-of-uncertainty overlay. The cone is the public face of the kind of ensemble spread Palmer's EPS first delivered for medium-range forecasting in November 1992. Image: NASA, public domain.*

### B.6 Bred-vector growth rates in the Lorenz system -- NEW DOWNLOAD
- Source page: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Bred_vector_growth_rates_in_Lorenz_system.png

- Caption: *Growth rates of bred vectors in the Lorenz system, from a paper co-authored by Kalnay -- a direct descendant of the breeding method NCEP would deploy on 7 December 1992 to compete with Palmer's singular-vector EPS. Image: Lynch et al. 2016, CC BY 3.0.*
