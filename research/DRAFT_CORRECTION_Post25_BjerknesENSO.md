### 9. "Eady-Holmboe analysis" attribution (line 58)

**Current text:**
> Holmboe's 1945 paper on baroclinic instability (known to every graduate student as the Eady-Holmboe analysis) was one of the foundational works of mid-latitude dynamical meteorology.

**Recommended replacement:**
> Holmboe and Bjerknes co-authored the 1944 paper "On the theory of cyclones," and Holmboe later mentored Jule Charney, whose 1947 PhD-derived paper on baroclinic instability — together with Eric Eady's independent 1949 derivation — established the foundational works of mid-latitude dynamical meteorology.

**Reason:** "Eady-Holmboe analysis" is not a standard textbook label for baroclinic instability. The canonical attributions are **Charney (1947)** and **Eady (1949)**. Holmboe's most famous instability work is the **Holmboe instability** (1962), a stratified shear flow phenomenon distinct from baroclinic instability. The Bjerknes-Holmboe 1944 paper on cyclones is real, but is not the "Eady-Holmboe analysis."

**Source:** Holmboe Wikipedia article lists his actual instability work; NAS Bjerknes memoir credits Charney with "the first mathematical solution describing growing waves on a baroclinic current."
