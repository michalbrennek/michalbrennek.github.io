## Smagorinsky biographical — ALL VERIFIED
- Joseph Smagorinsky born 29 January 1924 in New York City. (Wikipedia, Encyclopedia.com, Franklin Institute)
- Died 21 September 2005, aged 81, Hillsborough, NJ, after Parkinson's. (Princeton News obituary, patch.com)
- Parents from Gomel, Belarus — fled pogroms. (Wikipedia: "His parents were from Gomel, Belarus, which they fled during the life-threatening pogroms.")
- Studied at Brown then MIT during army service. Instructor at MIT was Ed Lorenz. (Wikipedia)
- NYU: BS 1947, MS 1948, PhD 1953 under Bernhard Haurwitz. (Wikipedia, Encyclopedia.com)
- Wife Margaret (née Knoepfel) — first woman statistician hired by Weather Bureau, ENIAC team member. (Wikipedia, Margaret Smagorinsky obituary in Princeton Patch)
- Director of GFDL 1955-1983 (~28 years, retired January 1983). (Wikipedia)

## GFDL founding — VERIFIED
- Founded 1955 as **General Circulation Research Section** of US Weather Bureau, at von Neumann's instigation, under Smagorinsky.
- Founding location: **Suitland, Maryland**, near JNWP unit. (Sloan/U Michigan center_descriptions/i.GFDL.html: "initially located in Suitland, Maryland (near the Weather Bureau's JNWP unit), later moved to Washington, D.C.")
- Renamed General Circulation Research Laboratory 1959.
- Renamed Geophysical Fluid Dynamics Laboratory (GFDL) 1963.
- Moved to Princeton's Forrestal Campus 1968.
- **NOT VERIFIED**: specific date "23 October 1955" — only year is confirmed. Post does not mention a specific date in 1955, only the year. No issue.

## Manabe biographical — VERIFIED with one minor note
- Syukuro Manabe born 21 September 1931, Ehime Prefecture, Japan.
- Birthplace name: Wikipedia, Japan Policy Forum, and post all use "**Shinritsu Village**" (now part of Shikokuchuo City). The official Nobel Prize biographical uses "Shingu Village" — likely the same place under a different romanization or post-merger naming. Post's "Shinritsu Village, Ehime Prefecture" is acceptable.
- University of Tokyo: BA 1953, MA 1955, **DSc 1958** (consensus from Wikipedia, Britannica, Grokipedia, Japan Policy Forum). Nobel Prize biographical says DSc 1959, but this is the outlier. Post's 1958 is consistent with consensus.
- Arrived USA 1958, formally joined General Circulation Research Section 1959 (per Sloan/U Michigan, confirmed). Post is correct: "arrived in Washington, D.C. in 1958 and formally joined the General Circulation Research Section in 1959."
- Worked at GFDL/Weather Bureau lineage until 1997. (Nobel Prize, Wikipedia)
- Returned to Japan 1997-2001, Frontier Research System for Global Change, directed Global Warming Research Division. (Nobel Prize, Wikipedia)
- Returned to Princeton 2002. (Nobel Prize, Wikipedia)
- Father and grandfather were physicians running village clinic. (Japan Policy Forum, Nobel biographical)
- Was 90 years old at Nobel announcement. Born Sep 1931, awarded Oct 2021. (Verified)

## Nobel Prize 2021 — VERIFIED
- Date announced: 5 October 2021. (Princeton News)
- Citation: "for the physical modelling of Earth's climate, quantifying variability and reliably predicting global warming." (Nobel Prize official)
- Recipients: Manabe and Hasselmann shared 1/2 of prize (each 1/4), Parisi received the other 1/2. Post's framing "one half ... to Manabe and Hasselmann, the other half ... to Parisi" is correct.
- Quote: "I did these experiments out of pure scientific curiosity. I never realized that it would become a problem of such wide-ranging concern for all of human society." — verified via Princeton News.

## Key scientific papers — VERIFIED
- **Manabe & Wetherald (1967)**, "Thermal Equilibrium of the Atmosphere with a Given Distribution of Relative Humidity," *J. Atmos. Sci.* 24(3), 241-259. (Wikipedia, ADS, Carbon Brief)
  - Voted most influential climate paper in Carbon Brief survey (8 nominations). VERIFIED.
  - CO2 doubling result 2.36°C (300→600 ppm, from Table 5). Post correctly quotes 2.36°C. VERIFIED via climategraphs analysis.
- **Manabe & Bryan (1969)**, "Climate Calculations with a Combined Ocean-Atmosphere Model," *J. Atmos. Sci.* 26(4), 786-789. First coupled ocean-atmosphere GCM. VERIFIED via AMS, IEEE ETHW PDF, NOAA.
- **Manabe & Wetherald (1975)**, "The Effects of Doubling the CO2 Concentration on the Climate of a General Circulation Model," *J. Atmos. Sci.* 32(1), 3-15. ~3°C average warming, with Arctic amplification due to snow/ice retreat. VERIFIED via AMS abstract listing.
- **Smagorinsky (1963)**, "General Circulation Experiments with the Primitive Equations: I. The Basic Experiment," *Mon. Wea. Rev.* 91(3), 99-164. 9-level hemispheric primitive-equation GCM on IBM 7030 Stretch. NOT DIRECTLY VERIFIED in this session (AMS returned 403); but post's description matches widely-known facts.

- Worked at GFDL with Manabe for decades. Co-authored 1967 and 1975 CO2 papers.

- 1979 NAS Ad Hoc Study Group on Carbon Dioxide and Climate, chaired by Jule Charney.
- ECS estimate ~3°C ± 1.5°C (range 1.5-4.5°C).

## ENIAC 1950 team — STANDARD CONSENSUS
- Published participants: Charney, Fjortoft, von Neumann; co-workers Smagorinsky, Platzman, Freeman; Margaret Smagorinsky and others as computer operators.
- Post correctly lists Charney, Fjortoft, Freeman, Platzman, Smagorinsky.

## Smagorinsky 1963 paper details — STANDARD CONSENSUS
- 9-level hemispheric primitive-equation GCM, IBM Stretch (7030). Stretch was world's fastest 1961-1964. (Standard fact.)
- Introduced Smagorinsky subgrid-scale turbulence closure model. (Standard fact.)
- Subsequent extension by Lilly and Deardorff into Smagorinsky-Lilly LES model — standard.
