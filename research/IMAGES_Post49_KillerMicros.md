# IMAGES — Post 49 "The Attack of the Killer Micros"

Image research for the MPP-vs-vector transition post (killer micros, MPI, spectral-transform parallelisation).

All images below were licence-verified on their Wikimedia Commons file pages. Six were downloaded to
`/home/michal/repos/michalbrennek.github.io/assets/images/`. Two desired diagrams (domain-decomposition /
halo-exchange, and a TOP500 architecture-share chart) were **NOT** found freely licensed on Commons — see the
"Not found / to create" section.

## HEADER IMAGE (downloaded)

**`Post49_Intel_Paragon_ORNL_header.jpg`** — landscape, public domain, an actual 1994 MPP machine.

The Intel Paragon XP-S-150 at Oak Ridge National Laboratory (1994). A real distributed-memory massively-parallel
machine of the killer-micros era (i860 microprocessors in a 2-D mesh), photographed as a wide machine-hall row —
public domain (US Government / DOE work), so no attribution obligation and safe to crop for a teaser.
