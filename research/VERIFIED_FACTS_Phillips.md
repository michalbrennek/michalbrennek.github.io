## Recommendations for Post 43

1. **Use the verified dates only.** Birth 9 July 1923. Death 15 March 2019 at Grace House, Windham, NH. Marriage to Martha Nissen in 1945.

2. **NMC retirement year:** do not assert a single year. Use "retired in the mid-1980s" or "from his full-time NMC role around 1984, continuing in research affiliation through 1988." The published sources are inconsistent and a single number cannot be defended.

3. **Do not claim an AMS "Norman Phillips Award" exists.** It does not appear on the 2024 or 2025 AMS award rolls.

4. **Do not list IMO Prize or Symons Gold Medal among his honours.** He did not receive either.

5. **Phillips 1959 NCI paper:** cite as "An example of non-linear computational instability" in the Rossby Memorial Volume (B. Bolin ed., Rockefeller Institute Press, 1959), pp 501-504. The Rossby Medal citation language explicitly names this work as a permanent contribution.

6. **The Charney-Phillips 1953 paper** is the canonical publication of the two-layer model from Phillips's thesis. The thesis itself was 25 pages and did not appear separately in a journal under another title.

7. **The 1957 sigma-coordinate paper** was originally in Journal of Meteorology vol 14, pp 184-185 (two pages) -- the journal was renamed Journal of the Atmospheric Sciences in 1962, so the paper is now indexed in AMS systems under JAS.

8. **Hybrid sigma-pressure coordinates** are Simmons and Burridge 1981 (MWR 109, 758-766), not "Simmons-Strufing."

9. **MIT chair sequence** is firm: Houghton (-1970), Phillips (1970-1974), Charney (1974-1977), Lorenz (1977-1981).

10. **World Weather Building** dedicated 22 October 1974; forecaster occupation completed "by January 1975" per the NOAA Magazine; the specific "19 February 1975" date in the research files lacks a publicly verified primary URL and should be softened to "early 1975" unless the writer can locate one.
