## Notes & flags (no correction required)

### Manabe's birthplace spelling
- Post uses "Shinritsu Village, Ehime Prefecture."
- Wikipedia, Japan Policy Forum, Britannica, and Grokipedia all support this.
- The official Nobel Prize biographical instead spells it "Shingu Village, Uma District, Ehime Prefecture."
- These are the same place — the area is now part of Shikokuchuo City (formed by 2004 merger). Both names appear in legitimate sources. The post is consistent with the majority of secondary sources and Manabe's own interview phrasing. **No correction required.**

### Manabe DSc year
- Post says DSc 1958.
- Nobel Prize biographical says 1959; all other primary and secondary sources (Wikipedia, Britannica, Grokipedia, Japan Policy Forum, the Hawaii IPRC profile, Sloan/U Mich) say 1958.
- Post is consistent with the majority and with the Sloan archive's specific claim that he formally joined GCRS in 1959 (after arriving 1958). **No correction required.** This is one of those cases where the official Nobel page itself appears to be inaccurate; the post is on solid ground.

### Optional: footnote 3 URL precision
