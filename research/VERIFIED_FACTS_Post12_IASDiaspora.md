## Post claims to verify

### IAS Report and Architecture
- Burks, Goldstine, von Neumann 1946 "Preliminary Discussion" 42 pages
- No patent, mailed freely; AEC/Army/Navy funding

### MANIAC I (Los Alamos, 1952)
- Construction started mid-1949
- First ran March 1952
- 40-bit words, Williams tubes, parallel architecture
- Metropolis named it
- Klara von Neumann programmed ENIAC and IAS
- Metropolis algorithm 1953 by Arianna Rosenbluth
- FPUT problem - Fermi, Pasta, Ulam, Tsingou
- 1956 chess on 6x6 board no bishops by Paul Stein and Mark Wells
- Retired 1958

### JOHNNIAC (RAND, 1954)
- Willis Ware engineer
- Used Selectron tubes - only IAS-family machine to use them
- 200 Selectrons made
- Keith Uncapher 10 hours error-free
- Selectrons replaced March 1955 with cores
- Logic Theorist 1955-1956 Newell, Shaw, Simon
- Ran on JOHNNIAC August 1956
- 1956 Dartmouth Conference
- JOSS in 1963
- 10 simultaneous users by 1966
- Retired February 18, 1966
- 51 349 hours of operation

### ILLIAC I and ORDVAC (1952)
- Two of every component clause
- Ralph Meagher chief engineer
- Abraham Taub lab head
- 40-bit words, 1024-word Williams tube memory
- First two computers that could exchange programs
- Validation took one week
- Lejaren Hiller and Leonard Isaacson - Illiac Suite 1956
- August 1956 first three movements performed
- Donald Gillies and James Snyder Sputnik orbit, 2 days, published Nature November 1957
- ILLIAC retired 1962, ORDVAC retired 1962

### WEIZAC (Weizmann Institute, 1955)
- Chaim Leib Pekeris
- Gerald Estrin lead, with wife Thelma Estrin
- Arrived 1954
- Components via Landseas Import-Export
- Copper strips from bicycle shop
- $50 000 budget = 20% of Weizmann annual
- First computer in Middle East
- First calculation late 1955
- Pekeris discovered amphidromic point in South Atlantic with M2 tide
- Retired 1963
- Gerald Estrin UCLA from 1956, taught Vint Cerf

### SILLIAC (Sydney, 1956)
- Harry Messel ran School of Physics at 30
- John Blatt physicist
- 2C51 valves from Bell Labs undersea repeaters - 6x more cost, 5x life
- Adolph Basser donated AU 50 000
- Final cost AU 75 000
- 2768 tubes
- Bob May (later Baron May of Oxford) first calculation
- 11 hours MTBF
- Retired 1968

### BESM (Moscow, 1952)
- Sergei Lebedev born Nizhny Novgorod 1902
- Moscow Higher Technical School 1928
- MESM at former monastery near Kyiv 1950-1951
- BESM-1 completed 1952
- 5000 vacuum tubes
- 8000-10 000 FLOPS
- 39 bits, three-address, floating-point, ferrite cores
- BESM-6 designed 1965, produced 1968-1987
- 355 units
- 60 000 transistors, 9 MHz, 1 MIPS
- 1975 Apollo-Soyuz - half an hour faster than NASA

### von Neumann biographical
- Born 1903 Budapest
- Died February 8, 1957 at Walter Reed at age 53
- Bone cancer, attributed to nuclear test radiation

- Pekeris was "educated as a meteorologist" - Post calls him "mathematician" (he led the Department of Applied Mathematics)
- Amphidromic point in South Atlantic - confirmed

- $50,000 = 20% of Weizmann Institute budget - confirmed Wikipedia [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WEIZAC]
- Operated until 29 December 1963
- Wikipedia: Estrin chosen to lead in 1952 (POST gets timeline ambiguous)

### von Neumann death

### Pekeris
- POST calls Pekeris "a mathematician at the Weizmann Institute of Science"
- IEEE ETHW source: Pekeris was "educated as a meteorologist" and led Department of Applied Mathematics
- More precisely, Pekeris was an applied mathematician and geophysicist with meteorology background. Calling him "mathematician" is defensible but oversimplifies.

## SUMMARY OF DISCREPANCIES

### Definite errors
1. **JOHNNIAC date** - POST table says "JOHNNIAC (1954)" but JOHNNIAC was operational in 1953
2. **ORDVAC tube count** - POST says ~2200; Wikipedia/manual says 2718 tubes (also reported as 2178 in some sources; not 2200)
3. **WEIZAC date of arrival** - POST says Estrins arrived 1954; they arrived in Haifa **26 December 1953** per IEEE primary source
4. **Klara von Neumann IAS programming** - POST claims she wrote code for "ENIAC and IAS machine"; her documented work is on ENIAC and MANIAC I (not IAS machine)

### Less serious / ambiguous
5. **Illiac Suite year** - POST says 1955 (composed) but Wikipedia categorizes it as "1957 composition" (completed)
6. **SILLIAC currency** - POST writes "AU 50 000" / "AU 35 200" / "AU 75 000" without the £ symbol; should be AU£ (Australian pound)
7. **Pekeris** - POST calls him "mathematician"; primary source says "educated as a meteorologist"
8. **BESM-6 clock** - POST says 9 MHz; Wikipedia article body says 10 MHz (infobox says 9 MHz)

### Stylistic / cosmetic
9. **MANIAC tubes** - Post says "~2500"; confirmed
10. **Adolph Basser** - POST says "Jeweler and horse-racing enthusiast"; widely-known fact but not in Wikipedia article on SILLIAC
11. POST claims "By 1956, ILLIAC I had more computing power than all the computers at Bell Labs combined" - Wikipedia: "in 1956, it had more computing power than all of Bell Telephone Laboratories" - confirmed
