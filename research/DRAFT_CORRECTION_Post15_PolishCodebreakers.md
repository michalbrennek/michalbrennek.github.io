## A. Errors of fact (should be fixed)

### A1. Poznan monument — "three pillars" is misleading

**Post says (intro to monument and image caption):**
> "Three bronze pillars, triangular in cross-section, each bearing the name of one of the three."

**Reality (verified by inyourpocket.com, Cryptologia 2008 announcement, photos on Wikimedia Commons):**
The monument is a **single triangular prism**, ~1.20 m wide on each side and 3.10 m tall, in bronze. One name is engraved on each of the three faces (Rejewski, Rozycki, Zygalski), surrounded by patterns of digits.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "Three bronze faces of a single triangular prism, each bearing the name of one of the three."
>
> Or simply: "A bronze prism, triangular in cross-section, with each face bearing the name of one of the three."

### A2. "211 British and 131 American" Bombes ≈ 340

**Post says:**
> "More than 340 Bombes were eventually built — 211 British and 131 American."

**Reality (Wikipedia "Bombe", Jennifer Wilcox NSA monograph):**
- British totals: ≈ 212 machines (73 standard + 68 new standard + 14 Jumbo + 57 Mammoth)
- US Navy: 121 (production stopped September 1944)
- US Army: a small number of 10-machine sets used at Arlington Hall, not a 131-machine count
- The combined "more than 340" overstates the total; ~336-340 is a closer fair estimate.

**Suggested rewrite:**
> "Roughly 340 Bombes were eventually built — 211 British and 121 American (US Navy), with a smaller US Army contribution."
>
> Or simpler: "More than 200 British Bombes and over 100 American machines were eventually built, the bulk of them in factories in Letchworth and Dayton, Ohio."

## B. Imprecisions worth tightening

### B1. "Born in 1909 in Olszana" for Rozycki

The post gives only year. Date of birth was **24 July 1909**. Adding the day, as the post does for Rejewski (16 August 1905) and could for Zygalski (15 July 1908), would make the three biographical openers consistent.

Suggested update:
> "Jerzy Rozycki was born on July 24, 1909, in Olszana (now Vilshana, Ukraine)."
> "Henryk Zygalski was born on July 15, 1908, in Poznan."

### B2. "Pyry, south of Warsaw" / "Kabaty Woods"

The post says "the Kabaty Woods at Pyry, south of Warsaw." The GCHQ page calls the site "Pyry Forest," and the Polish cipher centre there was codenamed Wicher. Pyry is in the Ursynow district adjoining the Kabaty Forest, so the description is acceptable, but a tighter phrasing would be:

> "...a secret conference was held at the Polish cipher centre in Pyry Forest, south of Warsaw."

### B3. "Polish-French-British" PC Bruno staffing

The post implies PC Bruno was Franco-Polish; Wikipedia describes it as a Polish-French-Spanish signals intelligence station. This is a minor point but worth a footnote if precision matters.

### B4. Schmidt's secrets — exact 1932 monthly key tables

The post asserts "operating manuals, monthly key tables for September and October 1932." Wikipedia (Schmidt article) and most Western sources are less specific about the exact months; the September/October 1932 specificity comes from Kahn (1996, *The Codebreakers*) and Kozaczuk (1984). Recommend adding a footnote to one of those, since the claim is specific and falsifiable.

### B5. "Returning from the Algiers branch office" — ship route

Post says: "the French passenger ship SS Lamoriciere departed Algiers bound for Marseille... Rozycki was aboard, returning from the Algiers branch office where he had been supervising the reading of encrypted German traffic."

This is correct (Algiers→Marseille), but the post never clarifies that he was on his way back to Cadix (near Uzes, in Vichy France), not Marseille itself, as his final destination. Wikipedia explicitly says he was returning to "the Cadix center... from a stint at Cadix's branch office at the Château Couba on the outskirts of Algiers." This adds clarity if you want to keep it.

## C. Unverified or weakly sourced

### C1. Rejewski died "while returning from shopping in Warsaw"

This detail (consistent with Kozaczuk's biography) is not in the Wikipedia article. It is plausible but uncited. Either footnote to Kozaczuk or drop the "while returning from shopping" gloss.

### C2. WETTERVORHERSAGE as the Bombe crib example

Post says Turing's Bombe "used cribs — guesses of known plaintext phrases like 'WETTERVORHERSAGE' (weather forecast)." Weather-report cribs were certainly used at Bletchley, but the specific token "WETTERVORHERSAGE" doesn't appear in the Bombe Wikipedia article; the canonical examples there are "WETTERBERICHT" (weather report) and the predictable opening "AN X" / "EINS." Either weaken to "...phrases like the daily weather report" or cite a specific source.

### C3. "Cipher Bureau evacuation September 1939 to France then UK"

This is the general arc and is correct, but the post compresses it: the actual route was Poland → Romania → France (PC Bruno, October 1939) → Vichy France (Cadix, from June 1940 after PC Bruno was evacuated 10 June 1940) → Spain (early 1943, after Vichy collapse Nov 1942) → UK (Rejewski and Zygalski reached London 3 August 1943). The post tells this correctly in "The Fates" section but doesn't note the Romania transit. Minor.

### C4. Grand Cross of Order of Polonia Restituta (2000) and British War Medal (2005)

The post lists these among posthumous recognitions but does not cite them. Both are widely reported (Wikipedia and Polish presidential decrees confirm the 2000 Grand Cross). Optional to footnote.

### C5. Zygalski's exact honorary doctorate year (1977)

Wikipedia just says "shortly before his death" (he died 30 August 1978). Polish sources (csenigma.pl) confirm 1977. If you want to keep the specific year in the post, a footnote to csenigma.pl or Kozaczuk would be helpful.
