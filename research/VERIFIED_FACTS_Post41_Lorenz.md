## Items flagged for caution / inheritance notes

1. **"James Van Vleck" as Nobel laureate** - The post says "Saunders Mac Lane, Marshall Stone, and James Van Vleck (the last of whom would later win the Nobel Prize in physics)" at Harvard. This is faithful to Emanuel NAS memoir p. 4, which writes "James Van Vleck (who was later awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics)". However, the 1977 Nobel laureate in Physics is **John Hasbrouck Van Vleck** ("John H. Van Vleck"), not James Van Vleck. James Van Vleck (1812-1875) was a 19th-century mathematician at Wesleyan/Yale. This is an Emanuel error inherited into both `research/people/Edward_Lorenz.md` and the post.

   - **Recommendation**: Either footnote that Emanuel's NAS memoir uses "James" but the Nobel laureate at Harvard at that time was John Hasbrouck Van Vleck (a position-of-honour issue, since correcting Emanuel mid-narrative is delicate), OR write the corrected name "John Van Vleck" (or "John Hasbrouck Van Vleck") with a footnote acknowledging Emanuel's NAS spelling as a primary-source quirk.

2. **Sanders 1980 "bomb" paper had John R. Gyakum as co-author.** The post says "Fred Sanders... who would coin the term 'bomb' for explosively deepening extratropical cyclones in his 1980 paper". This is not strictly wrong (Sanders was first author), but acknowledging Gyakum is more accurate. Optional refinement only.

3. **The Cessna monoplane at East Boston Airport.** The post-caption says Rossby's 1928 founding came "with Guggenheim Fund backing and a Cessna monoplane for upper-air observations from East Boston Airport". The Cessna acquisition was actually 1931 per MIT history sources (Daniel Sayre flew it for six months). The caption could be read as conflating 1928 founding with 1931 aircraft purchase. Minor only.

4. **Cancer cause for Charney.** Post says "Charney died from lung cancer on 16 June 1981". Wikipedia says simply "cancer" at the Sidney Farber Cancer Institute. The lung-cancer specification appears in several sources but not in all. Minor; date is correct.

5. **"Stommel... shared the world with Lorenz until 1978".** Stommel left MIT for WHOI in 1978, returning to physical oceanography full-time. The post statement is correct in spirit. Minor.

6. **Margaret Hamilton "arrived at MIT in the summer of 1959".** Wikipedia says "mid-1959". Consistent.
