## Confirmed facts

### Akio Arakawa
- Born **20 July 1927** (confirmed via en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akio_Arakawa). Post says "July 20, 1927" - CORRECT. (Task watch-list note said "July 22" which is wrong.)

### Yale Mintz
- Born **30 March 1916, Manhattan, New York** (palevsky.org genealogical record).
- Died **1991 in Jerusalem**, but exact date is disputed: UCLA obituary header says **April 27, 1991**; palevsky genealogical record says **May 27, 1991**. (Post 23 uses April 27 from the UC In Memoriam.)
- B.A. Dartmouth College 1937 (general humanistic studies); M.S. Columbia 1942 (geology); PhD UCLA 1949, the **second** UCLA Meteorology PhD ever (WebSearch summary).
- Hired UCLA 1950 (WebSearch summary).
- Retired UCLA **1977** and joined NASA Goddard as consulting scientist (WebSearch summary).

## Discrepancies / Issues found

### MINOR: "Panel of eight scientists" - actually 9
The post says "Charney assembled a panel of eight scientists: himself as chair, D. James Baker... Carl Wunsch of MIT -- and, as the sole representative of the atmospheric-modelling community, Akio Arakawa."
- Counting: Charney + Baker + Bolin + Dickinson + Goody + Leith + Stommel + Wunsch + Arakawa = **9 people**.
- Wikipedia Charney Report confirms 9 panel members.
- Either "eight scientists" should be "nine scientists", or the sentence should say "Charney assembled a panel of nine scientists" or "Charney assembled himself plus eight scientists".

### MINOR: Mintz Manhattan / Brooklyn birthplace
- Post says "Yale Mintz was born in Manhattan on March 30, 1916." This is CORRECT per genealogy.palevsky.org which confirms "Manhattan, New York, New York, USA".
- Task watch-list said "Brooklyn 1916" which is INCORRECT - post is right with Manhattan.

### CONFLICT: Mintz death date
- Post says: "He died there on April 27, 1991". This matches the UCLA In Memoriam 1993 (the cited source).
- Palevsky genealogical record gives "27 May 1991".
- Multiple secondary sources (Wikipedia Charney Report search, AMS Rossby page summary) confirm April 27, 1991.
- AMS Journal of Climate 1996 Johnson-Arakawa tribute (paywalled, not fetched) is the primary scientific tribute and would likely confirm.
- VERDICT: Post's April 27, 1991 stands by weight of authoritative sources. The May 27 date in Palevsky may be an error.

### MINOR / acceptable: Arakawa birth date discrepancy with task watch-list
- Task watch-list said "born 22 Jul 1927 Tokyo".
- Wikipedia Akio Arakawa confirms "July 20, 1927".
- Post says "born in Tokyo on July 20, 1927" - CORRECT.
- The "July 22" in the task watch-list appears to be a typo or error in the briefing.

### MAJOR FACT ERROR: Conway Leovy was NOT a UCLA graduate student
- Post: "Mintz began a parallel project with a UCLA graduate student named Conway Leovy."
- Reality: Conway Leovy got his PhD in Meteorology from **MIT in 1963** (per UW Atmos in-memoriam). Before joining UW in 1968, he was a **research meteorologist at the RAND Corporation in Santa Monica, CA** (per UW Atmos in-memoriam; also confirmed by NASA Mars history page).
- He collaborated with Mintz while at RAND, not as a UCLA grad student.
- "Conway Leovy went on to lead atmospheric sciences at the University of Washington" - he joined UW 1968, Full Professor 1974-1999. He was a leader in the field, though "lead atmospheric sciences" overstates if interpreted as chair.
- Their key paper: Leovy & Mintz, "Numerical Simulation of the Atmospheric Circulation and Climate of Mars," J. Atmos. Sci. **26(6), 1167-1190, 1969** (Wikipedia / AMS / NASA Mars history).

### MAJOR FACT ERROR: Vivian Lamb description wrong
- Post: "his collaborator Vivian Lamb -- a UCLA computer scientist who was not, despite the name, the atmospheric modeller"
- Reality: Vivian R. Lamb was at the **UCLA Department of Atmospheric Sciences** - she was an atmospheric scientist/modeler. She co-authored the seminal 1977 Arakawa-Lamb grid paper and the 1981 Arakawa-Lamb shallow-water enstrophy paper as a co-equal scientific collaborator. (Semantic Scholar, AMS Journals, Springer chapter abstracts all list her as "Department of Atmospheric Sciences, University of California, Los Angeles".)
- The parenthetical "not, despite the name, the atmospheric modeller" appears to confuse Vivian R. Lamb with someone else.

### LIKELY ERROR: IBM 709 at "UCLA Business School"
- Post: "weekend access on borrowed time from the UCLA Business School's IBM 709"; "The 709 had 32 000 words of core memory"
- Per Vast Machine UCLA chapter (WebSearch summary): "The UCLA Medical School had an IBM 7090 computer and it was idle during the weekends. Yale Mintz had an arrangement with the Medical School staff..."
- Discrepancies: machine was **IBM 7090** (not 709), location was **UCLA Medical School** (or Western Data Processing Center) - not Business School.
- IBM 7090 had 32,768 words of 36-bit core memory, so "32,000 words" is approximately right for the 7090 too.
- Note: The Western Data Processing Center at UCLA installed IBM 7090s in 1961, so the WDPC is another plausible location. The "Business School" attribution does not match any source I found.

### MINOR: Panel "of eight scientists"
- Post counts 8 panel members but actually names 9 (including Charney as chair). Wikipedia lists 9 panelists. Recommendation: change "eight" to "nine".

### UNVERIFIED but plausible
- Arakawa birthplace (post says Tokyo) - all secondary sources say "Japan" without specifying city. AIP oral history is the primary source. Consistent with Arakawa's high-school fire-warden duty in Tokyo described in the oral history.
- "Hidetoshi Arakawa (no relation)" - Hidetoshi Arakawa was a real JMA meteorologist; this is plausible.
- 1965 UCLA permanent return and family details (4-year-old son).
- The Mintz-Leovy Mars paper(s) "first described in papers in the mid-1960s and formalised through the late 1960s" - the formal 1969 JAS paper exists; earlier RAND research memorandum (RM-5110, 1966) confirms mid-1960s research.
