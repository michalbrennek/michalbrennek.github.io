## High-level verdict on Post 29

**Net assessment:** The post is mostly accurate on its core narrative (CDC 1604 specs, delivery to NPS Monterey January 1960, Cray as architect, ERA→Sperry→CDC genealogy, FNWF/FNMOC continuity, NCAR-CDC 3600 myth correctly debunked). The Cray bio is largely correct (Battle of the Bulge confirmed, BSEE/MS Minnesota confirmed, Sept 28 1925 birth confirmed, Oct 5 1996 death confirmed). The Norris bio is mostly correct (born Nebraska, ERA 1946, CDC 1957, died 2006). The Mullaney bio appears correct based on available sources.

**Major errors that must be fixed before publication:**
1. **CDC-IBM 1973 settlement was ~$80M, not $600M** (Coda section — order-of-magnitude error)
2. **CDC 1604 announcement venue: NOT WJCC 1959 Los Angeles** — WJCC 1959 was San Francisco in March; CDC 1604 announced Oct 16 1959 most likely via press release
3. **Cray's father: civil engineer, not "city engineer"** (per Wikipedia)
4. **FNWF named in 1961, not 1959** — naming history elision
5. **Cray-3 (Graywolf) was a CCC product, one unit delivered to NCAR, not "cancelled"** (the post conflates Cray Research with Cray Computer Corporation)

**Minor errors / imprecisions:**
6. "Westinghouse-trained" for Norris is misleading; he was University of Nebraska 1932
7. "$300,000" Cray Research seed money is disputed; Wikipedia says $250,000
8. "first fully transistorised scientific computer" overstated; "first commercially successful transistor scientific computer" more accurate
9. BESM-6 "CDC compatibility" claim is unverified in Wikipedia BESM-6
10. Cray accident: Sept 22 1996 (Sunday), not "22 or 23"
11. IBM 709 "contemporary" with 1604 is muddled — 7090 was contemporary (and transistor)
