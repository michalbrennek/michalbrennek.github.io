## WATCH-LIST ITEMS (asked for in the brief but NOT in the post)

The brief asks about operational NWP at each centre through 1960s: Met Office, ECMWF predecessors, JMA, FNOC, AFGWC, and the transition from barotropic to PE. The post mentions:

- **NMC/JNWPU** (centred): correctly covered.
- **UK Met Office**: only via Sutcliffe's 1956 quote. The Met Office did not get its first operational NWP until **1965** (on a Ferranti Mercury / English Electric KDF9, leading to a primitive equation model on an IBM 360/195 in 1972). The post does not claim anything wrong about the Met Office.
- **ECMWF predecessors**: ECMWF was founded only in 1975 — there are no "ECMWF predecessors" in the 1960s, since ECMWF is itself a 1970s creation. The post correctly omits this.
- **JMA (Japan)**: First operational NWP in **1959** on an IBM 704; barotropic. The post does not mention JMA — no error.
- **FNOC (US Navy Fleet Numerical Weather Central)**: Established 1961 Monterey; CDC 6500 in 1967. The post does not mention FNOC — no error.
- **AFGWC (US Air Force Global Weather Central)**: AFGWC was a Cray site (per memory: never CDC). The post does not mention AFGWC — no error.
- **Mintz-Arakawa 1960s**: UCLA general-circulation models, not operational at any NWP centre in the 1960s; the post does not mention them — no error.
- **Smagorinsky 1963 / Charney 1961-1969 / Lorenz 1963**: covered in adjacent posts (Posts 8, 16); only briefly alluded to here. No error.

The post correctly stays focused on the NMC story and Sutcliffe's quote as the British counterpoint.
