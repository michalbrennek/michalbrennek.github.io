## C. DO NOT USE -- tempting but legally unsafe

### IAS Archives photos (Shelby White and Leon Levy Archives Center, Princeton)
- IAS holds many photographs of von Neumann, Bigelow, Goldstine, Charney, Phillips at the machine -- including the 1952 dedication group photos, the Charney-Smagorinsky-Phillips-Lewis-Gilbarg-Platzman group in front of the computer (photographed by Joseph Smagorinsky himself), etc. **All require written permission from archives@ias.edu** unless they have been individually uploaded to Commons. Do not lift from albert.ias.edu or ias.edu directly. The two-man and four-man IAS-machine photos that ARE on Commons (Oppenheimer_and_von_Neumann... and Julian_Bigelow.jpg) are the only safe IAS shots and we already have them.

### AIP Emilio Segrè Visual Archives -- Charney_Jule_D1
- The famous 1952 Smagorinsky-shot photo of "the Meteorology Project at IAS" (Charney + Phillips + Lewis + Gilbarg + Platzman in front of the machine) is held by AIP ESVA. **Not on Commons**, not free-licensed. AIP's standard image license requires per-use permission for publication. This would have been the dream image -- showing Phillips alongside Charney at the IAS machine in 1952, exactly the moment the post pivots on -- but it is **not available** for free reuse. Do not lift from AIP. Mention in text that the photo exists, with a citation to AIP ESVA, but do not embed.

### MIT Museum / MIT News press photos of Phillips and Charney
- MIT Museum holdings and MIT News press images are not released under CC unless individually tagged. The MIT Museum's portrait of Phillips at his desk, the MIT-era photo of Charney at the blackboard, the "Chaos and Climate" symposium 2018 photos -- all press-use-only. **The Jule_Gregory_Charney.png file IS in this category but Wikimedia accepts it under PD-US-1978-89 because it was published in MIT promotional material 1978-89 without copyright notice** -- so for that specific file the PD claim survives Commons review. Do not generalize -- other MIT Museum photos of Phillips are not safe.

### AMS Bulletin obituary photos of Phillips
- BAMS obituaries are AMS copyright (post-1977). Not reusable.

### The 1956 QJRMS Phillips paper figures
- The original Figure 1 grid layout, Figure 2 model schematic, and the cyclone-development figures from "The general circulation of the atmosphere: a numerical experiment" (QJRMS 82, 1956) are RMS copyright. **Do NOT scan or reuse.** This is the same prohibition that applies to AMS journal figures pre-2014.

### Henry Hinds Laboratory (UChicago)
- Built 1968-69. Phillips was a grad student 1946-51, before this building existed. Using a Henry Hinds photo for the Chicago section would be a factual error. Use Rosenwald Hall (1916) or Reynolds Club / Ryerson (1894) instead -- those are buildings Phillips would actually have walked through.

### Getty Images / Alamy / commercial stock of Phillips, Charney, IAS, MIT, NMC
- All commercial. Not Commons. Not reusable.

### CHM (Computer History Museum) catalog photos
- The same rule as for the Cyber 205 post (Post 42) applies: CHM photos of IAS-machine artifacts at the museum are NOT free-licensed even though the artifacts themselves are PD-by-age. Do not lift from computerhistory.org. Use Wikimedia uploads only.

## D. Verification checklist before publishing

For each image you embed, the final caption block in the Markdown post should include:

1. The author or photographer name (exact spelling).
2. The license tag (CC BY-SA 4.0 / CC BY 4.0 / PD-USGov / PD-Harris-Ewing / PD-US-1978-89 / etc.).
3. For PD images, the basis (PD-old / PD-USGov / PD-no notice).
4. For CC images, the version number (3.0 vs 4.0) -- they ARE different licenses.
5. A pointer to the Commons file page (helpful for readers who want to verify).

Run before publishing:
```bash
# Confirm all referenced images exist locally
grep -oE '/assets/images/[a-zA-Z0-9_.\-]+' _posts/<your-post>.md | sort -u | while read f; do
  test -f ".$f" || echo "MISSING: $f"
done
```
