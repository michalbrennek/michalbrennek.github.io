## Errors found and corrections proposed

### 1. IMO Prize laureate paragraph — MAJOR ERRORS

**Current text (post §"The Rossby Medal and the IMO Prize"):**

> "The list of previous IMO laureates includes Carl-Gustaf Rossby (1956), Sverre Petterssen (1962), Tor Bergeron (1966), Reginald Sutcliffe (1971), Bert Bolin (1981), Lennart Bengtsson (1990), Tim Palmer (2007 -- shared the year with Shukla), Akio Arakawa (2008), Syukuro Manabe (2009), and Jule Charney (posthumously, 1980)."

**Per the official WMO IMO Prize laureate list and Wikipedia:**

- Rossby received the IMO Prize in **1957** (not 1956). The 1956 award (the first) was to Sir Nelson K. Johnson.
- Reginald Sutcliffe received the IMO Prize in **1963**, not 1971.
- Lennart Bengtsson received the IMO Prize in **2006**, not 1990.
- Tim Palmer received the IMO Prize in **2023**, not 2007, and did NOT share with Shukla. Shukla received it in 2007 alone (the 52nd IMO Prize).
- Jule Charney received the IMO Prize in **1971**, during his lifetime (Charney died June 1981). The "posthumously 1980" attribution in the post is wrong on two counts.
- Akio Arakawa and Syukuro Manabe are not confirmed as IMO Prize laureates in the sources searched. Their respective AMS honours (Rossby Medal, etc.) are real but the IMO Prize years (2008, 2009) given in the post are not verified.

**Proposed correction:** Rewrite the paragraph using only verified laureates, e.g.:

> "Shukla's IMO Prize made him the 52nd laureate of the WMO's highest honour, joining (among others) Carl-Gustaf Rossby (1957), Tor Bergeron (1966), Jule Charney (1971), Bert Bolin (1981), Lennart Bengtsson (2006), and Tim Palmer (2023). The IMO Prize is given annually since 1956 for outstanding contributions to international meteorology."

### 4. Shukla 1975 paper misattributed to GFDL post-doc work

**Current text (§ "GFDL Princeton, 1976-1979"):**

> "The GFDL work produced two consequential papers. **Shukla 1975**'s *Journal of the Atmospheric Sciences* paper..."

**Issue:** Shukla 1975 was published in 1975, BEFORE his GFDL post-doctoral fellowship began in 1976. The paper used the GFDL atmospheric model but the work itself was done during Shukla's MIT doctoral years. Title: "Effect of Arabian Sea-Surface Temperature Anomaly on Indian Summer Monsoon: A Numerical Experiment with the GFDL Model."

**Proposed correction:**

> "The GFDL period was the consolidation of a research line Shukla had begun at MIT. His **Shukla 1975** *Journal of the Atmospheric Sciences* paper "Effect of Arabian sea-surface temperature anomaly on Indian summer monsoon: A numerical experiment with the GFDL model" -- the first quantitative GCM study of how a specific oceanic boundary condition could be tied to a specific atmospheric outcome -- had been the formative paper of his MIT doctoral years; he extended its programme at GFDL on the TI ASC. The 1976-1979 GFDL period also produced his collaboration with Yale Mintz at UCLA that culminated in **Shukla and Mintz 1982**'s *Science* paper..."

### 5. Education dates inconsistent with sources

**Current text:**

> "He took his Bachelor of Science in Geology and Geophysics in 1962-1965, and his Master of Science in 1965-1967."

**Per Wikipedia and GMU Support Science feature:**

Shukla earned his BSc (honors) at BHU at age 18 (i.e., 1962), and his MSc in Geophysics from BHU in **1964**, then began doctoral work that resulted in the BHU PhD in 1971. The post's "BSc 1962-1965, MSc 1965-1967" framing is inconsistent with sources that say MSc 1964.

**Proposed correction:**

> "He took his Bachelor of Science in Physics, Mathematics, and Geology at BHU at age eighteen, completing it in 1962, and his Master of Science in Geophysics in 1964. He then began doctoral work at BHU under senior Indian meteorologists, and was awarded the BHU PhD in 1971 as an external candidate."

(Also update the figure caption to "BSc completed 1962, MSc 1964, doctoral candidate 1965-1971" or similar.)
