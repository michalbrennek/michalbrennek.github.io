# Kalnay's Later Career — Maryland 1999–2024

Agent #4 research for Post 46. Scope: post-NCEP life. Bred vectors operationalisation, Maryland chair, LETKF, the 2003 textbook, and death 13 August 2024.

## 1. Bred vectors becoming operational at NCEP

- **Method paper**: Toth, Z., and E. Kalnay, 1993: *Ensemble Forecasting at NMC: The Generation of Perturbations*. **BAMS 74:2317–2330**.
- **Operational start**: **7 December 1992** at NCEP (then still partly called NMC).
- **Initial configuration**: T62L18 (~210 km horizontal resolution); the first operational global ensemble system included **two perturbed pairs (4 perturbed members) in addition to the control**, running once daily from 00Z to 12-day lead.
- **The 14-forecasts-per-day count** comes from later: lagged-average forecast technique combined with perturbed pairs and control to reach the operational 14 member count. (NOT 16 — this was corrected via Post 45 verifier.)
- **March 1994 expansion**: ensemble expanded to **seven independent breeding cycles**.
- Bred vectors are the American operational answer to ECMWF's singular-vector EPS (Post 45). The intellectual contrast — finite-amplitude growing modes vs. tangent-linear optimal perturbations — drove a decade of comparative predictability studies.

### Toth's later trajectory

- Stayed at NCEP EMC through 2000s, led NCEP global ensemble and NAEFS (North American Ensemble Forecast System) collaboration with Canadian Meteorological Centre + Mexican NMS.
- Moved to **NOAA/OAR/ESRL Global Systems Division (Boulder)**, where he became Senior Scientist in the Global Systems Laboratory specialising in predictability of weather and climate.
- Not director of ESRL — senior scientist position.

## 2. Joining Maryland and the chair years

- Joined **University of Maryland AOSC (Department of Atmospheric and Oceanic Science)** in **1999** as **Chair**, succeeding the previous chair.
- Held concurrent appointments in IPST (Institute for Physical Science and Technology) and CSCAMM (Center for Scientific Computation and Mathematical Modeling).
- **Chair tenure ended before 2007** — James Carton appointed chair in 2007. So chair tenure was roughly **1999–2007** (Wikipedia does not list explicit end date; the post-2007 transition to Carton is documented in AOSC self-study).
- **2001**: named **Distinguished University Professor** (UMD's highest academic rank).
- **2008**: selected as the first holder of the **Eugenia Brin Endowed Professorship in Data Assimilation** (the chair was named for her — an unusual honor of a chair named after a still-active scholar at her home institution).

## 3. The Weather/Chaos Group and LETKF

- Co-founded the **Weather/Chaos Group** with mathematician **James A. Yorke** (Yorke had co-coined the term "chaos" in 1975 — Li & Yorke).
- Core LETKF collaborators at UMD: **Brian R. Hunt**, **Eric J. Kostelich** (Arizona State, frequent collaborator), **Istvan Szunyogh**, and student **Takemasa Miyoshi**.
- **Anchor paper**: Hunt, B. R., E. J. Kostelich, and I. Szunyogh, 2007: *Efficient data assimilation for spatiotemporal chaos: A local ensemble transform Kalman filter*. **Physica D 230:112–126**.
- LETKF: ensemble Kalman filter that performs the analysis update **locally** (in observation space, in patches surrounding each grid point), making it tractable for global NWP and embarrassingly parallel.
- Operational deployment at multiple centers since ~2010 (JMA, NOAA, KMA, NASA tests). Miyoshi went on to lead operational LETKF at JMA and later at RIKEN.

## 4. The 2003 textbook

- **Kalnay, E., 2003**: *Atmospheric Modeling, Data Assimilation and Predictability*. **Cambridge University Press**, xxii + 341 pp. ISBNs 0 521 79179 0 (hardback), 0 521 79629 6 (paperback). Published October 2002 / 2003 depending on edition.
- Reviewed by **Andrew Lorenc** (the OI/3D-Var/4D-Var theorist himself) in **QJRMS 2003** — a notable signal: the leading authority on DA reviewed the canonical DA textbook.
- Universally regarded as **the** graduate text for atmospheric DA and predictability. The fact that Kalnay wrote it while a department chair, having just finished a decade as EMC Director and a definitional reanalysis paper, marks a remarkable career compression.

## 5. PhD students

- **Mentored, advised, or co-advised 45 PhD students** over her career.
- **13 of her PhD advisees became faculty members** nationally and internationally (per AMS obituary, Sep 2024).
- Best-known advisees include: **Zoltan Toth** (bred vectors, NCEP/ESRL), **Istvan Szunyogh** (LETKF, Texas A&M), **Brian R. Hunt** (LETKF — strictly speaking a UMD math collaborator, not Kalnay's student), **Takemasa Miyoshi** (RIKEN, operational LETKF), **Shu-Chih Yang** (National Central University, Taiwan).

## 7. Death — 13 August 2024

- **Confirmed date: 13 August 2024**, aged 81. (Wikipedia + AMS Headlines obit "Trailblazer meteorologist Eugenia Kalnay (1942–2024)" published 14 September 2024.)
- **NOT 24 July 2024** — corrected once already via Post 45 verifier.
- The first-ever Shukla Earth System Predictability Prize (2024) reads in retrospect like a valedictory recognition.
- A **University of Maryland memorial celebration** was held at the Memorial Chapel (Sep 2024 timeframe).
- AMS published "In Memoriam: Eugenia Kalnay" in Feb 2026 (formal memorial piece, distinct from the Sep 2024 news-side obituary).
- The Sep 2024 AMS obit described her as one who "made highly consequential theoretical and practical contributions to numerical weather prediction and predictability, and who was a beloved mentor to generations of students."

## 8. Citation impact of Kalnay et al. 1996 (for cross-reference)

- **~34,024 citations** as of 2025/2026 (Semantic Scholar / aggregator data).
- One of the most highly cited papers in atmospheric science, regularly cited as **the** most-cited or among the top three (alongside Mauna Loa CO2 dataset papers and IPCC working-group reports).
