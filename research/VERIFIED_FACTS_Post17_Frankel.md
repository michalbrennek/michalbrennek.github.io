## Verified Facts (post is correct on these)

- Stanley Phillips Frankel born 1919 Los Angeles (post says June 6 1919, sources confirm 1919)
- Died May 1978 at age 58 (sources confirm)
- PhD from UC Berkeley
- Post-doc under Oppenheimer starting 1942 (sources confirm)
- Worked with Eldred Nelson on neutron diffusion / critical mass
- Arrived Los Alamos early 1943 (post says 1943 - confirmed)
- Wife Mary Frankel involved in computing pool
- Marchant and Friden desk calculators used
- Recognized IBM tabulators could be programmed (PCAM machines)
- Caught "computer disease" - arctangent story confirmed by Feynman
- Feynman took over IBM operation
- August 1945 traveled with Nick Metropolis to Moore School (ENIAC) - confirmed
- ENIAC: 30 tons, 40 panels, 17468 tubes (the figures are standard)
- Half a million punch cards - confirmed
- ENIAC calculation revealed flaws in Teller's Super; Teller's spring 1946 report
- 1947 paper with Metropolis on computational methods
- Berni Alder collaboration at Caltech (1949-50)
- 1950 Manchester Mark 1 trip, paper published 1955 in J Chem Phys
- Lost security clearance early 1950s red scare (sources say 1949 plausible)
- Designed MINAC at Caltech (1954) - 113 vacuum tubes + diodes
- LGP-30 first manufactured 1956 by Librascope
- Royal McBee/Royal Precision joint venture for sales
- Designer with James Cass (Caltech grad student)
- ~500 units sold (320-493 actually manufactured)
- $47,000 price
- ~800 pounds
- Drum 6.5 inches diameter, 7 inches long
- 4096 word memory, 64 tracks of 64 words
- Flexowriter for I/O
- LGP-21 transistorized successor
- Oppenheimer security clearance: suspended Dec 21 1953, hearings April 1954, revoked June 29 1954 (well-established history)

## DISPUTED / INCORRECT CLAIMS in the post

### CRITICAL ERROR 1: Group T-5 vs T-6
The post says: "Frankel created **Group T-5**: teams of women..."

This is WRONG on two counts:
- T-5 was the desk-calculator HUMAN computing pool, led by Donald (Moll) Flanders, formed summer 1943
- Frankel and Nelson led **T-6 (IBM Computations)**, formed September 1944 when IBM Computations were removed from T-2 (Serber's Diffusion Theory group)
- Mary Frankel set up problems for the T-5 pool to calculate but she was not just a "wife"; she was a key staff member
- The "teams of women" calculating with Marchants/Fridens were T-5 under Flanders

Source: Haigh, Priestley, Rope arXiv 2103.05705 (peer-reviewed authoritative source on Los Alamos computing infrastructure).

### CRITICAL ERROR 2: LGP-30 word length
The post says: "4096 words of 32-bit memory"
Correct: 31-bit (the 32nd bit was a spacer on drum, always zero in memory)
Sources: Wikipedia LGP-30, Masswerk

### CRITICAL ERROR 3: ENIAC calculation date
The post says: "in November 1945 ran the calculation... results were available by December"
Correct: The calculation was RUN in December 1945 and January 1946 (not November-December)
ENIAC was first put to work on December 10, 1945 on the Los Alamos problem.
Programming was designed in fall 1945 after August arrival.
Sources: Multiple, including APS, vanemden, Computing and Manhattan Project (AHF).

### CRITICAL ERROR 4: Frankel moved aside timing
The post says: "Frankel was moved aside" after Feynman took over.
Correct: Frankel was moved to Teller's F-Division in January 1945 (because of "computer disease")
He was NOT moved aside vaguely after Feynman - the specific date is January 1945.
This actually makes the chronology cleaner: Frankel moved to F-Division Jan 1945, then was already with Teller's group when he and Metropolis went to ENIAC in August 1945 to do the Super calculation. The post buries this connection.
Source: Haigh et al.

### MODERATE ERROR 5: Adjacent address time
The post says: "The time between adjacent addresses was approximately 2.34 milliseconds"
The LGP-30 drum rotated at 3700 rpm (~16.2 ms/revolution); maximum access time per revolution was 17 ms; quick access registers were 0.26 ms. The "2.34 ms" figure is not in standard sources I checked. Possibly the average access time using interlace addressing, but should be verified or removed.

### MINOR ISSUE 6: PhD year
The post does not give a year but implies Frankel had PhD by 1942 (when he started post-doc with Oppenheimer). Wikipedia and AHF confirm postdoc began 1942 but neither gives PhD year explicitly. The user-prompt watch-list says "UC Berkeley PhD 1942 under Oppenheimer" - but sources don't explicitly state 1942, only that postdoc began 1942.

### MINOR ISSUE 7: Frankel "supervised" the calculation
The post says: "supervised by a 26-year-old physicist from Los Angeles"
26 fits: born June 6 1919, by November-December 1945 he was 26. But "supervised" oversells - he and Metropolis jointly designed and ran it.

### POSSIBLE ERROR 8: 1949 clearance loss date
The post says: "In early 1949, Stanley Frankel lost his security clearance"
Sources only confirm "early 1950s red scare." The 1949 date is plausible but I cannot find a primary-source confirmation in the time available. Should be presented less definitively or sourced.
