## Lawrence (Larry) McMillin
- NOAA/NESDIS scientist, Camp Springs, MD. Pioneer of satellite retrieval and the SPLIT-WINDOW technique.
- Split-window (SW) approach first proposed by **McMillin (1975)** for sea-surface-temperature retrievals. McMillin & D. S. Crosby (1984): theory/validation of multiple-window SST technique.
- Early VTPR-era work: NOAA Technical Report NESS 65 (McMillin et al.) on satellite infrared soundings from NOAA spacecraft — VTPR retrievals. Published Monthly Weather Review 1978; TOVS retrieval algorithm developer.
- Canonical split-window citation (well-known in the field; NOT personally fetched — verify before printing): **McMillin, L. M., 1975: "Estimation of sea surface temperatures from two infrared window measurements with different absorption," J. Geophys. Res. 80:5113-5117.**
- McMillin & Crosby 1984 (multiple-window SST); McMillin & Dean; McMillin was on the NOAA/NESS team producing operational VTPR soundings (NOAA-2 onward) and TIROS-N/TOVS retrieval algorithms (1979).
- FLAG / NOT VERIFIED: birth/death dates. The June 2024 Tezak obituary (Joliet, IL; Caterpillar assembler b.1946) is a DIFFERENT Lawrence J. McMillin, NOT the NOAA scientist. Do not use it. ## John R. Eyre
- Met Office (Exeter) and formerly ECMWF. Pioneer of DIRECT RADIANCE ASSIMILATION via 1D-Var.
- Key paper: **Eyre, J. R., 1989: "Inversion of cloudy satellite sounding radiances by nonlinear optimal estimation"** (QJRMS). The 1D-Var retrieval scheme (Eyre 1989) used NWP short-range forecasts as background — the intermediate step towards direct radiance assimilation.
- Wrote major two-part history: **Eyre et al. (2020)** and **Eyre 2022 "Assimilation of satellite data in numerical weather prediction. Part II: Recent years"** (QJRMS, doi:10.1002/qj.4228). Part I also exists. These are prime narrative sources.
- Eyre, English & Forsythe 2020 (Atmos context).

## Erik Andersson
- ECMWF data-assimilation scientist; central to 3D-Var and 4D-Var implementation and to TOVS/radiance assimilation.
- **Andersson et al. 1991** (with A. Hollingsworth, G. Kelly, P. Lönnberg, J. Pailleux & Z. Zhang) — biases in retrieved data / OI+TOVS.
- **Andersson, Pailleux, Thépaut, Eyre, McNally, Kelly & others 1994** — "Use of cloud-cleared radiances in three/four-dimensional variational assimilation" (QJRMS) — landmark direct-radiance-in-variational paper.
- Andersson & Thépaut 2008: "ECMWF's 4D-Var data assimilation system – the genesis and ten years in operations" (ECMWF Newsletter).

## Roger Saunders
- Met Office (Bracknell then Exeter). Head of Satellite Section at ECMWF **1995-1999**, responsible for new satellite data types in the NWP model; took scientific lead on ongoing development of RTTOV fast radiative transfer model.
- RTTOV = development of a fast RT model for TOVS originally developed at ECMWF in the early 1990s. Now maintained under EUMETSAT NWP SAF (Satellite Application Facility). Author of successive RTTOV Users Guides (RTTOV-7 at Met Office Bracknell; RTTOV-9 at Exeter).
- Lead author **Saunders et al. 2018, "An update on the RTTOV fast radiative transfer model (currently at version 12)"**, Geoscientific Model Development 11:2717 (doi:10.5194/gmd-11-2717-2018). Co-authors: Hocking, Turner, Rayer, Rundle, Brunel, Vidot, Roquet, Matricardi, Geer, Bormann, Lupu.

## Sean Healy
- ECMWF scientist; leading figure in GPS/GNSS radio-occultation assimilation at ECMWF.
- Healy 2001: "Radio occultation bending angle and impact parameter errors caused by horizontal gradients in the troposphere: a simulation study".
- **Healy, Eyre, Hamrud & Thépaut 2007**: "Assimilating GPS radio occultation measurements with two-dimensional bending angle observation operators" — established the operational bending-angle operator approach.
- Showed CHAMP RO measurements contain useful T information in upper-trop/lower-strat. Led ECMWF's start of COSMIC-2/FORMOSAT-7 assimilation (25 March 2020).
- FLAG: birth date not found (living).

## Christian Rocken
- UCAR scientist; central to GPS/MET and COSMIC radio-occultation data processing at the COSMIC Data Analysis and Archive Center (CDAAC).
- **Rocken et al. 1997**: statistical validation of GPS/MET — compared 1200+ soundings of troposphere/stratosphere to correlative datasets (JGR). Foundational RO validation paper.
- US-side project scientist for COSMIC-2 partnership at UCAR.
- FLAG: birth date not found (living; German name, likely German-born — NOT VERIFIED).

## Richard (Rick) Anthes
- Born **March 9, 1944**. BS, MS, PhD in meteorology, University of Wisconsin-Madison.
- 1971: joined Pennsylvania State University (full professor 1978). 1981: director of NCAR's Atmospheric Analysis and Prediction Division. 1986: director of NCAR. **1988-Jan 2012: President of UCAR** (longest-serving to that date).
- 1993: became interested in radio occultation; key player in proof-of-concept **GPS/MET** experiment and in **COSMIC** (Constellation Observing System for Meteorology, Ionosphere and Climate) — joint Taiwan/US, six satellites launched **15 April 2006**.
- Sources: Wikipedia; UCAR archives (b.1944); NCAR distinguished-scholar CV (has full birthdate/place).

## Carla Cardinali
- ECMWF scientist; developed and led adjoint-based Forecast Sensitivity to Observations (FSOI) at ECMWF.
- **Cardinali, C., 2009: "Monitoring the observation impact on the short-range forecast," QJRMS 135:239-250 (doi:10.1002/qj.366).** KEY RESULT for the post: largest single contribution to decreasing 24h forecast error is **AMSU-A (~25%)** — quantified proof AMSU-A is the single most valuable observing system. Analysis over summer 2006 + winter 2007.
- Adjoint-based FSOI implemented operationally at ECMWF June 2012, run daily since.
- FLAG: birth date not found (living, Italian).

## Rolf Langland
- US Naval Research Laboratory (NRL), Monterey, CA.
- **Langland, R. H. & N. L. Baker, 2004: "Estimation of observation impact using the NRL atmospheric variational data assimilation adjoint system," Tellus A 56:189-201.** Introduced the adjoint-based FSOI method — computationally cheap, partitions impact by instrument/variable/region/level. Used NOGAPS 24h/30h forecast error, NAVDAS, June & Dec 2002.
- FLAG: birth date not found.

## D. P. (Dick) Dee
- ECMWF; "reanalysis pioneer." Inventor/implementer of VARIATIONAL BIAS CORRECTION (VarBC) for satellite radiances.
- Joined ECMWF as visiting scientist **2003-04** to develop VarBC; recruited into Sakari Uppala's ERA-Interim reanalysis team because the reanalysis needed automated bias correction.
- **Dee, D. P., 2004: "Variational bias correction of radiance data in the ECMWF system,"** Proc. ECMWF workshop on assimilation of high-spectral-resolution sounders in NWP, Reading, 28 June-1 July 2004. VarBC operational 2006.
- **Dee & Uppala 2009: "Variational bias correction of satellite radiance data in the ERA-Interim reanalysis," QJRMS** (doi:10.1002/qj.493).
- Lead author of the landmark **Dee et al. 2011, "The ERA-Interim reanalysis," QJRMS** (covers 1979-2019). Bowed out 2019 as ERA5 replaced ERA-Interim.
- (Note: Auligné, McNally & Dee 2007 is the other canonical VarBC paper — adaptive bias correction for satellite data.)
- FLAG: birth date not found (living). Dutch/American name.

## Cross-link / framing notes for the writer
- FRAME paper (SH catches up): Simmons & Hollingsworth 2002 (QJRMS) — same verification paper as Post 47; anchors the two-hemisphere skill-convergence chart.
- Already-introduced people to cross-link: Jean-Noel Thepaut + Anthony Hollingsworth (Post 47), Andrew Lorenc (Post 44), Eugenia Kalnay (Post 46).
- Key quantified payoff for the ENGINE argument: Cardinali 2009 — AMSU-A ~25% of forecast-error reduction (single most valuable observing system). Langland & Baker 2004 introduced the adjoint FSOI method NRL used to reach the same conclusion.
- RTTOV as the enabling "software machine": originated at ECMWF early 1990s (Eyre's fast-RT work), Saunders took scientific lead (ECMWF Satellite Section head 1995-99), now NWP SAF; Saunders et al. 2018 GMD documents version history.
- Direct-radiance turn chronology: Eyre 1989 (1D-Var) -> Andersson et al. 1994 (cloud-cleared radiances in variational) -> ECMWF 3D-Var 1996 -> 4D-Var 1997 -> VarBC (Dee 2004, operational 2006).
