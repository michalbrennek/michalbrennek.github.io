### 6. `NMC_surface_analysis_1974-04-04.png` -- THE flagship image for the post

- **What it shows:** An actual NMC surface analysis chart for 4 April 1974 at 00Z. Isobars, fronts, the works -- exactly the kind of operational analysis OI was created to produce.
- **Commons file:** File:Sfc1974040400z.png
- **Direct URL:** https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f1/Sfc1974040400z.png
- **License:** Public domain (PD-USGov-NOAA)
- **Author/source:** National Meteorological Center / NOAA Central Library microfilm.
- **Date:** 4 April 1974 (uploaded to Commons 2 April 2014).
- **Why it suits the post:** This is the most important image. The chart shows analysed isobars and frontal positions -- which is precisely what OI produces (analysed fields at grid points blending background with observations). The 1974 date predates operational OI at NMC (1978) by four years, so caption it as the pre-OI hand-analysed era OR as illustrative-of-the-product. Either framing works.
- **Suggested caption:** *"Analiza synoptyczna z poziomu morza wykonana przez National Meteorological Center, 4 kwietnia 1974, 00Z. Tego rodzaju produkt -- siatka analiz z izobarami i frontami -- jest tym, co OI produkuje numerycznie, łącząc obserwacje z polem startowym. Materiały: NOAA Central Library, domena publiczna (PD-USGov-NOAA)."*

### 10. Mathematical diagrams for the B-matrix / correlation function section

Three viable Commons SVGs for illustrating the covariance / autocorrelation structure that OI estimates:

- **File:Gaussian_curve.svg** -- canonical bell curve. Use to illustrate Gaussian covariance kernel C(r) = exp(-r²/2L²).
- **File:Csv-2d-gaussian-multivarate-distributions.svg** -- 2D multivariate Gaussian with covariance matrix. Excellent for showing what "B matrix" means.
- **File:Gaussianprocess_gapMean.svg** -- Gaussian process posterior mean with uncertainty bands. This is mathematically the *same object* as OI: a regression with a Gaussian-covariance prior.

All three are standard Commons SVG-mathematics, CC BY-SA 4.0 or public domain (file pages would need quick verification of exact tag, but these are standard pedagogical diagrams uploaded over the past decade and are routinely safe).

**Suggested filename and caption for the most useful one (Gaussian_curve.svg):**
- Filename in repo: `Gaussian_covariance_kernel.svg`
- Caption: *"Funkcja Gaussa -- najprostszy model funkcji korelacji błędu pola startowego (background error correlation) używany w OI. Im dalej od punktu obserwacji, tym mniejsza waga -- macierz B w OI to po prostu siatka takich funkcji korelacji rozciągnięta na wszystkie pary punktów modelu. Diagram: Wikimedia Commons, CC BY-SA."*

## C. IMAGE ABSENCE NOTES -- these you cannot get from free sources

### NO FREE LORENC PORTRAIT FOUND
Andrew Lorenc has no portrait on Wikimedia Commons or any other free-license source I found. He is still living (retired Senior Research Fellow at the Met Office) and all available photos of him are either on Met Office Crown Copyright pages or in academic conference photo galleries with restrictive licensing. The Met Office's Open Government Licence v3 *would* be acceptable in principle, but I found no OGL-tagged Lorenc photograph in this research budget. **The post must work around Lorenc's absence the same way Post 42 worked around the missing Cyber 205 photo** -- name him in the text, link to his ResearchGate/Met Office profile, but do not embed an image.

### NO FREE HOLLINGSWORTH PORTRAIT FOUND
Anthony "Tony" Hollingsworth (1943-2007) died in 2007 in Ireland. ECMWF's obituary page (https://www.ecmwf.int/en/about/media-centre/news/2007/dr-anthony-tony-hollingsworth-1943-2007) contains a portrait but ECMWF web content is not CC-licensed. The WMO obituary page is also restrictive. **No Commons file exists.** Same workaround: name him, link to ECMWF obituary, no embedded image.

### NO FREE GANDIN PORTRAIT FOUND
Lev Gandin (1922-2009) emigrated from the USSR to the US in 1981, worked at NOAA NESDIS, died in 2009. His IMSC award page (https://imsc.pacificclimate.org/awards_gandin.shtml) commemorates him but is not CC-licensed. No Wikipedia article appears to have a portrait. Soviet-era photos of Gandin would in principle be useful but I found nothing on Commons. **The post must work around the absence.** This is the most important absence to acknowledge -- Gandin is the namesake of the post and has no face. Suggest the post lean on (a) the Voeikov observatory exterior, (b) the Saint Petersburg / Leningrad cityscape, (c) optionally a Gandin-IMSC-Award icon mention -- to give the section visual weight without a portrait.
