## HIGH-1: Silberman first name and institution

**Location**: line 68 of the post.

**Current text**:
> The first spectral barotropic experiment was due to the Princeton meteorologist **Leonard Silberman** in 1954.[^4]

**Problem**: The 1954 paper *Planetary Waves in the Atmosphere* (*Journal of Meteorology* 11: 27-34) was authored by **Isadore Silberman**, affiliated with **New York University**, not Princeton.

**Proposed correction**:
> The first spectral barotropic experiment was due to the NYU meteorologist **Isadore Silberman** in 1954.[^4]

Footnote 4 (line 316) is already correct as cited; no change needed there.

## LOW-3: NCAR CCM0A/B basis (no change recommended)

**Location**: line 156.

**Current text**:
> "the original versions of the NCAR Community Climate Model, CCM0A and CCM0B, were based on the Australian spectral model (Bourke et al., 1977)."

**Note**: The canonical NCAR phrasing is fuller: "based on the Australian spectral model and an adiabatic, inviscid version of the ECMWF spectral model." The post's truncation is defensible — it is specifically discussing the Australian propagation pathway — but a reader could mistakenly conclude CCM0 was exclusively Bourke-derived. Consider adding a parenthetical "(supplemented by an adiabatic, inviscid version of the ECMWF spectral model)" if precision matters.
