## Minor Discrepancy 1: Kasahara death date

**Post (line 147)**: "from 1963 until his death on **29 March 2022** at the age of ninety-five."

**Footnote 27 references**: UCAR News "NCAR and UCAR mourn the passing of pioneering scientist Akira Kasahara"

**Issue**: UCAR News article gives the death date as **30 March 2022**, not 29 March. This is a one-day discrepancy.

**Recommendation**: Either
- (a) Change "29 March 2022" to "30 March 2022" to match the UCAR institutional source cited; OR
- (b) Leave as-is if the author has primary source evidence (family obituary, Legacy.com page) supporting 29 March, but add a footnote acknowledging the variance.

The Legacy.com Daily Camera obituary referenced in footnote 27 returned HTTP 403 and could not be directly verified within budget.

## Minor Discrepancy 2: Mesa Lab ground-breaking date

**Post (line 16)**: "designed by **I. M. Pei** with ground broken on 9 June 1964 and dedicated on 10 May 1967"

**Wikipedia Mesa Laboratory says**: "In April 1964 the Martin K. Eby Construction Company broke ground on the lab" - i.e. construction began April 1964, not June.

**Possibilities**:
- "9 June 1964" may refer to a separate **groundbreaking ceremony** rather than the start of construction (these can be months apart).
- The author may have a more specific UCAR archival source.

**Recommendation**: Either:
- (a) Provide a footnote citation specifically for the 9 June 1964 date.
- (b) Soften to "ground broken in 1964 and dedicated on 10 May 1967".

The dedication date 10 May 1967 is more specific than Wikipedia ("dedicated in 1967") and is plausible; no contradicting evidence found.
