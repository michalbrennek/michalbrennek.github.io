## VERIFIED Cane-Zebiak 1996-97 forecast:
- The CPC Mar 96 bulletin (Zebiak & Cane): forecast "continuation of somewhat below normal Nino 3 conditions, with strongest negative anomalies for winter 1996-97" -- the POST quotes this almost verbatim and characterizes as "weak La Nina"
- The Mar 97 bulletin: predicted "below normal Niño 3 conditions for spring 1997, returning to normal by winter 1997-98 and becoming slightly warm in early 1998" -- this is a MORE NUANCED picture than the post's claim that "January 1997 forecast called weak La Nina" for what was then to come
- POST is broadly correct that Cane-Zebiak underpredicted at that time, but the "January 1997 forecast" framing is approximate - the actual published Mar 97 bulletin (which uses initial conditions through January 1997) was even by Mar 97 saying "slightly warm in early 1998"
- Caveat: this is acceptable for a popular post; the broader claim is supported by Barnston, Glantz, He 1999 and by Cane's own admission. The specific phrasing in the post is defensible.
