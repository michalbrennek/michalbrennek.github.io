## DISPUTED / NEEDS CORRECTION

### Major errors

1. **Carl-Gustaf Rossby's age at death**
   - Post says: "died suddenly in Stockholm on August 19, 1957, **at sixty-eight**"
   - ACTUAL: Rossby was born Dec 28, 1898, died Aug 19, 1957 — **age 58**, NOT 68.
   - Source: Wikipedia, multiple obituaries.

2. **Carl-Gustaf Rossby's nationality**
   - Post says: "Carl-Gustaf Rossby, **Norwegian-American**"
   - ACTUAL: Rossby was **Swedish-American** (born Stockholm; became US citizen 1938).
   - Source: Wikipedia, multiple sources.

3. **Bjørn Helland-Hansen's death date and age**
   - Post says: "died earlier in the year, on **April 22, 1957, at eighty**"
   - ACTUAL: Helland-Hansen born Oct 16, 1877, died **September 7, 1957, in Bergen — age 79**, NOT April 22 at age 80.
   - This also breaks post's narrative that he "died earlier in the year." He actually died LATER than both Rossby (Aug 19) and Sverdrup (Aug 21).
   - The "three Norwegians... dead within four months" claim becomes wrong (it should be "within about three weeks, all in August-September 1957").
   - Source: Wikipedia, Prabook, multiple sources.

4. **Jacob Bjerknes "president of the American Meteorological Society in 1959" — FALSE**
   - Post says: "He served as president of the International Union of Geodesy and Geophysics from 1948 to 1951 and of **the American Meteorological Society in 1959**."
   - ACTUAL: According to AMS Past Presidents Directory:
     - 1958-59: **Sverre Petterssen** (NOT Bjerknes)
     - 1960-61: Thomas F. Malone
     - 1962-63: Morris Neiburger
   - Jacob Bjerknes was NEVER AMS president. He was elected Honorary Member and Fellow of AMS in 1966.
   - Source: AMS official Past Presidents Directory.

5. **Jacob Bjerknes "president of the IUGG 1948-1951" — partly wrong**
   - Post says: "president of the International Union of Geodesy and Geophysics from 1948 to 1951"
   - ACTUAL per NAS memoir: He was "President, Meteorological Association, International Union of Geodesy and Geophysics, 1948-51" — i.e. president of IAMAS (now called the International Association of Meteorology and Atmospheric Sciences) WITHIN IUGG, NOT of IUGG itself.

6. **UCLA Meteorology Department founding date**
   - Post says: "He founded, at UCLA, the first American department of meteorology at a civilian university" and "The UCLA Meteorology Department that Jacob Bjerknes founded **in 1940**"
   - ACTUAL per NAS memoir: "In 1940 he joined the faculty of the University of California at Los Angeles as professor of meteorology and head of the **Section of Meteorology in the Department of Physics**. ... In 1945 a new Department of Meteorology was established at the University of California at Los Angeles, with Jack Bjerknes as chairman."
   - The Meteorology Department was established in **1945**, not 1940. Bjerknes headed a meteorology *section* of the Physics Department from 1940.
   - UCLA history page also says "department was founded in 1940 by J. Bjerknes, J. Holmboe, and J. Kaplan" — there's source ambiguity. The 1940 founding is sometimes called the "Section" (which became the Department in 1945). Post could clarify this.
   - Post also omits Joseph Kaplan as a co-founder.

7. **Mark Cane's move to Lamont-Doherty**
   - Post says: "Mark Cane had come to Lamont **in 1981** from MIT"
   - ACTUAL per Cane's CV: He was Assistant/Associate Professor at MIT 1979-**1984**. He joined Lamont-Doherty as Sr. Research Scientist in **1984**, not 1981.
   - Source: Mark Cane CV (Columbia Applied Physics).

8. **Holmboe-Eady analysis attribution**
   - Post says: "Holmboe's 1945 paper on baroclinic instability (**known to every graduate student as the Eady-Holmboe analysis**)"
   - ACTUAL: Baroclinic instability is canonically attributed to **Charney (1947)** and **Eady (1949)** — not "Eady-Holmboe." Holmboe's most famous instability work is on stratified shear flow (Holmboe instability, 1962). The Bjerknes-Holmboe 1944 paper "On the theory of cyclones" exists, but the "Eady-Holmboe" attribution as a standard textbook label is not correct. It was Charney who, as Bjerknes-Holmboe's first doctoral student (PhD UCLA 1946), produced "the first mathematical solution describing growing waves on a baroclinic current" per the NAS memoir.

### Minor issues / ambiguities

9. **Vilhelm Bjerknes Leipzig date**
   - Post says 1913. NAS memoir confirms 1913. (Wikipedia table shows 1912 — slight discrepancy in sources.) Post is OK on this.

10. **Wyrtki nationality "German-American"**
    - Post: "German-American oceanographer Klaus Wyrtki"
    - Wyrtki was born in Tarnowitz, Upper Silesia (then part of Germany, now Poland). Wikipedia describes him as American. Post's "German-American" is defensible historically.

11. **El Chichon eruption dates**
    - Post says "March 28 and April 4, 1982."
    - The eruption sequence BEGAN March 28 at 23:32 local; major Plinian eruptions were March 29, April 3, April 4.
    - Post's framing is acceptable as a date range, but could be more precise.

12. **Vilhelm Bjerknes wife/Jacob's grandmother**
    - Per NAS: Vilhelm married Honoria Sophia Bonnevie. So Jacob's mother was Honoria, daughter of Jacob Aall Bonnevie. Post mentions this implicitly through Jacob's full name "Jacob Aall Bonnevie Bjerknes." OK.

13. **"Department of Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences" name**
    - The UCLA atmos.ucla.edu history page calls it "Department of Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences" now. Post mentions UCLA Meteorology — that was its historical name. OK.
