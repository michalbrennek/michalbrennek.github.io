## Additional verified findings during this audit

- Carl Wunsch did NOT receive the Alexander Agassiz Medal. His major awards: Maurice Ewing Medal (1990), William Bowie Medal (2006), Henry Stommel Research Award (2000). Member of NAS since 1978.
- Joseph Smagorinsky did NOT receive the Alexander Agassiz Medal. His major awards: Carl-Gustaf Rossby Research Medal (1972), Buys Ballot Medal (1973), IMO Prize (1974), Benjamin Franklin Medal (2003).
- Jorge Sarmiento does NOT appear on the Wikipedia complete list of Agassiz Medal recipients.
- The Smagorinsky-Lorenz teaching connection is verified: Smagorinsky was a WWII Army Air Corps meteorology trainee at MIT in 1944, instructed by Edward Lorenz. (Source: NOAA 200th-anniversary Smagorinsky biography.)
- The IBM 7030 Stretch arrived at the U.S. Weather Bureau in June/July 1962 (per Wikipedia). The GCRL/GFDL borrowing-Stretch story in 1961 is therefore slightly anachronistic if read literally, but the lab had access to it by 1962-63 when Bryan's first paper was published.
- Gill & Bryan 1971 actual title: "Effects of geometry on the circulation of a three-dimensional southern-hemisphere ocean model," Deep-Sea Research vol. 18, pp. 685-721 (NOT the title given in the post's footnote 20).
- The Manabe-Bryan 1969 model coverage: 120° of longitude from pole to equator, i.e., one-sixth of Earth's surface. Not "realistic North Pacific and North Atlantic geometry."
- The 1969 coupled integration consumed about 1200 hours of UNIVAC 1108 CPU time (NOAA 200th-anniversary).
