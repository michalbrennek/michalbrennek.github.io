**Parallel-to-R1 framing**: ECMWF's ERA-15 prioritised *homogeneity within the satellite era* (1979 start = TIROS-N/TOVS + FGGE) over *length*. NCEP chose length (back to 1948 via NCAR's archive). Reading and Camp Springs collaborated — Per Kållberg sat on the NCEP/NCAR Advisory Panel, and Kalnay et al. 1996 thank "Rex Gibson and Per Kallberg, (ECMWF)" for observations. Friendly rivalry, not competition. (Source: BAMSPAPR.ASC acknowledgements; ECMWF Dussin et al. 2025 EArth and Space Science "Four Generations of ECMWF Reanalyses".)

## CROSS-SOURCE DISCREPANCIES (resolved)

1. **Death date — 13 August 2024 vs Academia Europaea's "14 August 2024"**
   - **Resolution: 13 August 2024.**
   - AMS Headlines obituary (14 September 2024) and Wikipedia both give 13 August. The Academia Europaea page (`ae-info.org/ae/Member/Kalnay_Eugenia`) is the lone outlier. AE entries are submitted/edited by members and the maintenance lag for deceased members is documented elsewhere; the AMS obit, written by the professional society and based on family/colleague input, is the primary source.
   - Post 46 writers: **use 13 August 2024**. The Post 45 verifier also independently locked this in.

2. **Jagadish Shukla Earth System Predictability Prize — 2023 (AE) vs 2024 (Wikipedia)**
   - **Resolution: 2024 (first-ever award).**
   - Re-verified this session: AMS announced the 2024 Weather, Water, and Climate Honorees press release names Kalnay as the **first recipient** of the new Shukla prize; the AMS prize landing page also confirms 2024. The award was presented at the AMS Annual Meeting in January 2024 (Baltimore).
   - Post 46 writers: **use 2024, first-ever recipient**. AE's "2023" is wrong (probably a nomination-year vs. presentation-year confusion).

3. **Symons Memorial Medal (Royal Meteorological Society)**
   - **Resolution: Kalnay did NOT receive the Symons Medal. Remove from any awards list.**
   - Re-verified this session against the RMetS Symons Gold Medal recipient list (Wikipedia mirror, 1902-2023): Kalnay's name does not appear. The first female recipient was **Sue Grimmond, 2020** — so the briefing's claim that Kalnay received the Symons Medal cannot be reconciled with the historical record.
   - Post 46 writers: **do NOT list Symons among Kalnay's awards.** Use IMO Prize 2009 as the international meteorological community's apex recognition of her.

4. **Bred vectors operational configuration — 16 members vs 14 forecasts/day**
   - **Resolution: 14 forecasts/day (NOT 16 members).**
   - Re-verified via Toth & Kalnay 1997 MWR paper search results: "single breeding cycle providing 5 global predictions (14 if lagged forecasts were also considered) valid to 10 days every day" — operational from 7 December 1992 at T62L18. The "16" number that some secondary sources use confuses (a) lagged-forecast count, (b) the post-March-1994 seven-cycle expansion, and (c) the 16-day lead time of the post-1994 system.
   - Post 46 writers: **use "14 forecasts per day" at T62L18 operational from 7 December 1992**; the March 1994 expansion to seven cycles is a separate paragraph.

5. **Maryland chair tenure — start 1999, end-year**
   - **Resolution: 1999-2007 (with caveat).**
   - Start year (1999) is confirmed by Wikipedia and the UMD Clark School bio. End-year (2007) comes from the AOSC self-study PDF that names Carton as Kalnay's successor. The AOSC department has had multiple chairs since (current chair S. Nigam per UMD AOSC website 2025-2026); the specific 2007 transition was not independently verifiable from public web sources this session. Cited as Medium confidence.
   - Post 46 writers: safe formulation is "Chair of AOSC from 1999 into the mid-2000s" if a single end-year would be too precise.

6. **CDAS launch year — Agent #3's "1989"**
   - **Resolution: CDAS project began 1990; the Advisory Committee operated from 1989.**
   - Re-verified this session via BAMSPAPR.ASC fetch: "The motivation for the CDAS project was..." and "The Advisory Committee, chaired by Julia Nogués-Paegle from 1989 to 1993..." — the Advisory Committee started 1989, CDAS itself 1990. Agent #3's "1989" conflated the two.
   - Post 46 writers: **CDAS began 1990; Advisory Committee 1989; April 1991 workshop opens the full NCEP-NCAR reanalysis project.**

7. **NMC → NCEP rename date — 1 October vs 5 October 1995**
   - **Resolution: 1 October 1995.**
   - Agent #3 wrote both "1 October 1995" (most places) and "5 October 1995" (one place) in the institutions file. The EMC official history page says "In October of 1995" without giving a calendar day; secondary sources and the Kalnay biography sources consistently use 1 October. Treat as 1 October unless a primary NOAA document specifying the calendar day surfaces.

8. **Citation count of Kalnay et al. 1996 — 29,937 (Agent #3) vs ~34,024 (Agent #4)**
   - **Resolution: both are aggregator snapshots at different dates; quote a range.**
   - Both numbers come from web aggregators (SciSpace ~29,937; Semantic Scholar ~34,024) at slightly different snapshot dates. Post 46 should write "well over 30,000 citations as of the mid-2020s" or "one of the most-cited papers in atmospheric science" without committing to a specific number, since Google Scholar (the canonical NWP-community count) was not retrievable this session.
