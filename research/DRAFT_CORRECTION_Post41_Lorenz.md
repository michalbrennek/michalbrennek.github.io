## 1. SHOULD FIX -- "James Van Vleck" -> "John Hasbrouck Van Vleck" (or footnote)

### Location

§1 "Hartford to Cambridge, 1917-1948", paragraph beginning "He went on directly to
Harvard University for graduate work in mathematics". Around line 61 of the post.

### Current text

> He studied group theory, set theory, and combinatorial topology under Saunders
> Mac Lane, Marshall Stone, and **James Van Vleck** (the last of whom would later
> win the Nobel Prize in physics).

### Problem

The 1977 Nobel Prize in Physics was awarded to **John Hasbrouck Van Vleck** (commonly
"John H. Van Vleck"), shared with Philip W. Anderson and Sir Nevill Mott. He taught at
Harvard from 1934 onward and was Hollis Professor of Mathematics and Natural Philosophy
from 1951. He was Lorenz's contemporary at Harvard during Lorenz's 1938-1942 graduate
years.

"James Van Vleck" appears to be an error. The post is faithfully reproducing the same
phrasing as Emanuel's NAS Biographical Memoir (p. 4: "James Van Vleck (who was later
awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics)"), but Emanuel made an error: there is no Nobel
laureate by that name. (There was a 19th-century mathematician James Van Vleck, but
he died in 1875, and he was at Wesleyan, not Harvard.)

### Recommendation

**Option A (minimal change, footnote correction)**:

Change the name to "John Hasbrouck Van Vleck" and add a footnote saying:

> Emanuel's NAS memoir gives the name as "James Van Vleck"; this appears to be a
> slip. The Harvard physics professor and 1977 Nobel laureate is John Hasbrouck Van
> Vleck (commonly "John H. Van Vleck").

**Option B (in-line correction)**:

> He studied group theory, set theory, and combinatorial topology under Saunders
> Mac Lane, Marshall Stone, and John Hasbrouck Van Vleck (the last of whom would
> later win the 1977 Nobel Prize in physics).

Option B is preferable because it removes the inherited error without dragging Emanuel
into a quasi-correction footnote.

## 3. OPTIONAL REFINEMENT -- Cessna at East Boston was 1931, not 1928

### Location

Figure caption following §1 (line 71 approximately).

### Current text

> The department was the first university programme of its kind in the United
> States; by the time Lorenz arrived for cadet training in 1942 Rossby had moved on
> to the University of Chicago, but the department was already in the hands of
> Henry Houghton, who would run it for twenty-five years.

Actually that text is fine. The earlier sentence is:

> Rossby had founded what would become the MIT Department of Meteorology in 1928
> inside the Department of Aeronautical Engineering, with Guggenheim Fund backing
> and a Cessna monoplane for upper-air observations from East Boston Airport.

### Refinement (optional)

The Guggenheim Fund backing is correct for the 1928 establishment. The Cessna
monoplane was actually acquired in 1931, when Professor Daniel Sayre flew it for six
months from East Boston Airport. The current phrasing reads as if all three (the
1928 founding, the Guggenheim backing, and the Cessna) were simultaneous. Optional
clarification:

> Rossby had founded what would become the MIT Department of Meteorology in 1928
> inside the Department of Aeronautical Engineering, with Guggenheim Fund backing.
> A Cessna monoplane for upper-air observations from East Boston Airport followed
> in 1931.

This is genuinely minor and probably not worth changing if it would break the rhythm.
